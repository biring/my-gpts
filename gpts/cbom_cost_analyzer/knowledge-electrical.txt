PURPOSE
This document defines how to break the Electrical Components category down into 15 fixed sub-categories and summarize them into a single table that reconciles back to the Electrical Components row in the top-level category table.

This document supplements the Core GPT Instructions with electrical-sub-category classification rules and examples. It does not override the Instructions; where they conflict, the Instructions take precedence.


SCOPE
Applies to:
- CBOM line items already classified as Electrical Components (per knowledge-category.txt).

Does not apply to:
- Line items classified under Mechanical Components, Bare Board, Assembly and Test, or Others.


CLASSIFICATION METHOD
For every Electrical Components line item, use the combination of Description, Manufacturer Name, and Manufacturer Part Number to search the internet and determine the correct sub-category. Do not rely on description text alone.

Steps for each line item:
1. Search the internet using: [Description] [Manufacturer Name] [Manufacturer Part Number].
2. Use the search results (datasheet, product page, distributor listing) to identify the component type.
3. Map the identified component type to the closest matching sub-category from the 15 sub-categories defined below. If the type is identified but no sub-category matches (e.g. a battery or antenna), apply the tie-break rules under DECISION CRITERIA; skip to step 5 only if the sub-category still cannot be assigned.
4. If search results are inconclusive or the part cannot be found, fall back to description-based matching using the DECISION CRITERIA below.
5. If the sub-category still cannot be determined after both internet search and description-based matching → assign to Other Electrical and flag in the Judgment Calls block with reason "type undetermined."


RULES
- Every Electrical Components line item must be assigned to exactly one of 15 sub-categories: Passive R/L/C (Commodity), Resistors (Specialty), Capacitors (Specialty), Inductors (Specialty), Diodes, Transistors, ICs, Connectors, Crystals & Oscillators, LEDs, Switches & Relays, Fuses & Protection, Sensors, Modules, Other Electrical.
- Output is a second table, shown below the top-level category table, with exactly three columns: Sub-Category, Count, Sum of Cost.
- Table has exactly 16 rows: one per sub-category (in the order listed above) plus a Total row. All 15 sub-category rows appear even when Count is 0.
- Count = number of line items assigned to that sub-category. A multi-quantity line item (e.g. qty 2) still counts as 1 line item, consistent with knowledge-category.txt.
- Sum of Cost = sum of extended cost for all line items assigned to that sub-category, rounded to 2 decimal places.
- Immediately below the table, report the Sub-Count Check and Sub-Cost Check using the format defined in knowledge-output.txt, reconciling against the Electrical Components row of the top-level table (not the whole CBOM).
- Distinguish rule-matched items from items routed to a sub-category only because no rule clearly applied. List the latter in the Judgment Calls block (see knowledge-output.txt) with reference and a one-line reason.


FORBIDDEN PRACTICES
- Do not leave any Electrical Components line item unassigned to a sub-category.
- Do not assign a line item to more than one sub-category.
- Do not invent a 16th sub-category.
- Do not apply the Specialty split to any family other than Resistors, Capacitors, and Inductors.
- Do not skip the Sub-Count Check / Sub-Cost Check lines, even when both pass; use the format defined in knowledge-output.txt.


DECISION CRITERIA (used when search is inconclusive, or when type is identified but no sub-category matches)
Passive R/L/C (Commodity): general-purpose fixed or SMD resistors ("popcorn parts") — standard tolerance, standard wattage (≤1W), no special temperature coefficient requirement; ceramic, electrolytic (<1000µF), tantalum, film, and mica capacitors; ferrite beads, signal inductors, standard chokes, and common mode chokes — general-purpose EMI filtering and signal-path parts.

Resistors (Specialty): any resistor meeting one or more of the following: current-sense, high wattage (>1W), tight temperature coefficient (≤100 ppm/°C), or high accuracy (≤0.1% tolerance).

Capacitors (Specialty): any capacitor meeting one or more of the following: supercapacitor/ultracapacitor (EDLC), electrolytic ≥1000µF, X-rated capacitor (line-to-line EMI suppression), Y-rated capacitor (line-to-ground safety), or other safety-rated capacitors.

Inductors (Specialty): transformers (isolation, flyback, forward, gate drive), custom-wound inductors, power inductors (SMPS/DC-DC converters), toroidal inductors, coupled inductors, and PFC inductors — power, energy storage, and isolation magnetics.

Diodes: standard, Zener, and Schottky diodes (signal/power, not LEDs).

Transistors: discrete only — BJT, MOSFET, FET, IGBT, SCR, Triac, Diac, and other discrete power switching semiconductors. Packaged multi-device power modules (e.g. half-bridge, full-bridge IGBT modules) classify as Modules, not here.

ICs: MCU, analog, logic, memory, regulators.

Connectors: board-mount headers, sockets, board-to-board/board-to-wire connectors.

Crystals & Oscillators: crystals, resonators, oscillator modules. Classify by primary function — a canned or MEMS oscillator module routes here, not to Modules.

LEDs: indicator and display LEDs (separated from signal/power diodes).

Switches & Relays: electronic or electromechanical switching devices.

Fuses & Protection: fuses, TVS diodes, MOV (metal oxide varistors), and ESD protection devices.

Sensors: electronic components or modules that detect, measure, or transduce physical conditions, including pressure, temperature, humidity, current, voltage, position, motion, optical, magnetic, gas, and flow sensors.

Modules: integrated electronic assemblies or packaged functional blocks that combine multiple components or circuitry into one purchased line item, including DC-DC converter modules, RF/wireless modules, hybrid circuits, and similar functional electronic modules. Does not include sensing modules (→ Sensors) or frequency-generation modules (→ Crystals & Oscillators).

Other Electrical: anything electronic not covered above (e.g. antennas, batteries).

Tie-breaks:
- A component matching two sub-categories by description (e.g. a relay described as a "switch") → use the more specific term in the description (Relay → Switches & Relays).
- Modules vs. ICs: if the purchased item is a bare IC in a standard package (DIP, SOT, QFN, BGA, etc.) → ICs. If it is a multi-component assembly or module-form PCB (castellated edges, shielded can, SIP with integrated passives) → Modules.
- Modules vs. Crystals & Oscillators: frequency-generation modules (canned oscillators, MEMS clock modules, TCXOs) → Crystals & Oscillators, not Modules.
- A sensing module (e.g. a packaged sensor with integrated signal conditioning) → Sensors, not Modules.
- If a part clearly belongs to Electrical Components but matches none of the other 14 sub-categories → Other Electrical, not a new category, and flag in the Judgment Calls block.
